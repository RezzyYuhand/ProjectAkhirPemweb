Deforestation in Indonesia hits record low, but experts fear a rebound

JAKARTA — Indonesia's deforestation rate hit a historic low in 2020, with the government crediting its various policies prohibiting forest-clearing, and experts attributing the trend to more rains, falling oil palm prices, and an economic slowdown as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. The country lost 115,459 hectares (285,300 acres) of forest cover in 2020, an area the size of Los Angeles. That's a 75% drop from 2019, according to the Ministry of Environment and Forestry. “In the past, we've often said that our deforestation [rate] was in the millions [of hectares],” said Belinda Arunarwati Margono, the ministry’s director of forest resource monitoring. But the 2020 deforestation rate, she said, “is remarkable for us because this is the lowest deforestation figure that we’ve ever achieved.”
